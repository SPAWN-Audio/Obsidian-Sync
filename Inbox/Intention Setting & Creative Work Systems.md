### 1. What This Video Is About

- Creative intention setting, personal goals, and the systems built to support them
- Framed as useful beyond creative work — but tested most thoroughly _within_ a creative process
- Also serves as Mickey's own brain-reset/clarification tool

---

### 2. Intentions vs. Goals — A Philosophical Shift

- Deliberately uses the word **"intention"** over "goal" — goals tend to be outcome-oriented, intentions are process-oriented
- Outcome-based thinking removes agency because so much is outside your control
- The trap: people get inspired by someone else's success story and start doing the work, but their _intention_ is still the outcome (the deal, the fame, the win) — not the daily doing

**References used to illustrate this:**

- **Alyssa Liu** — "I love struggling, actually. It makes me feel alive." Interpreted not as glorifying struggle, but as genuine acceptance of it as part of the process; she emanates being fully _in_ process
- **Timothée Chalamet's 2025 SAG speech** — casual comparison of actors to athletes pursuing "greatness"; critiqued as a very American, achievement-vehicle way to think about art
- **Noah Wyle video** — two career interruptions forced him to ask: _what do I want the daily work to actually feel like?_ Concluded he works for his own health and orientation, not money. Identified as someone visibly and authentically _in process_

---

### 3. How to Discover Your Intentions

- There's no Notion template for this — you're already wired to pursue what resonates
- **Scripting** — a manifestation-adjacent technique used here as a goal-setting tool: write your ideal future day in the present tense with gratitude, focusing on the _texture_ of daily tasks, not the outcomes
    - "I wake up and go to my studio, I begin painting at 9am" ✅
    - "I want to be in a gallery and famous" ❌
- The key question: what is the _tangible, present-moment quality_ of doing the thing — not what you imagine the thing to be from the outside

**Further references:**

- **Brian Ruttenberg / "White Hot Fire Inside of You"** — _"I don't make art, I make things."_ Removing preciousness keeps creativity from becoming timid; focuses attention on the actual object being made rather than its artistic legacy
- **Bread & Puppet "Why Cheap Art?" Manifesto** — art is not a privilege of museums and the rich; admired for being both a manifesto _and_ a creative act in itself; illustrates how abstract and personal intentions can legitimately be

---

### 4. Systems — Built Around _Your_ Life, Not Someone Else's

Core argument: **a system is only as good as how well it fits the actual functions of your life**

- References an interior design video: _"The functions of your life need to be fundamentally incorporated into the design, not squeezed in where it fits your vibe"_ — directly applied to creative systems
- **"The purpose of a system is what it does"** — heuristic from Stafford Beer; no point claiming a system does what it constantly fails to do
- Morning journaling: creates zen for one person, causes stress and lateness for another — same system, different results
- **Rachel Cusk on domesticity** — writers with elegant book-lined studies vs. choosing to work in the smallest, most uncomfortable room anyway; warns against obsessing over the _aesthetic_ of a process over its actual function
- **Brian Ruttenberg's lampshade to-do list** — literally tacks notes to a spinning lampshade; it's just a to-do list, but it works for him

**Twyla Tharp's Box Method** (from _The Creative Habit_, 2003) — presented as a solid foundational starting point:

- One physical box per project, filled with every piece of research, inspiration, notes, clippings, videos
- The box doesn't create — it's the repository of creative potential, not that potential realised
- Gives a sense of organisation and connection to a project even when direction is unclear
- Back-burnering projects is fine — the box holds them

### 5. Mickey's Personal System

**Intention setting process:**

- Annual audit across all life areas: work, finances, creativity, relationships, home, health
- Asks: where did I give/get too much or too little?
- Then does scripting for the coming year — focused on energy and quality of experience, not specific achievements
- Works backwards from intentions to identify what's needed (e.g. trust, safety, freedom of time, abundance)
- Maps out everything wanted for the year, organised loosely by quarter (Q1/Q2 more defined, Q3/Q4 looser)

**Day-to-day execution:**

- Monthly goals → weekly to-do list → completed items moved to the bottom
- Handwritten calendar (not synced to Google Calendar) — fills in dates by hand; the tactile, handmade quality matters to him
- Identifies as a **sprinter not a marathoner** — builds ramp-up and ramp-down time around intensive creative work

**Creative project process (follows Twyla Tharp logic):**

1. **Collect** — saves everything encountered (TikToks, Instagram, book pages, links) to **Sublyme** (sponsored tool — an infinite canvas / knowledge management app)
2. **Organise into collections** — vague, abstract collection names so they can become any format (video essay, play, film)
3. **Canvas** — builds a visual layout/flow chart from a collection's references before starting
4. **Draft** — begins writing/creating; if stuck, goes back to collect more; if overwhelmed, takes a break (walks, cleans a drawer)
5. **Arbitrary deadlines** — sets soft first-draft targets; treats himself "like a third grader" with a curriculum, taking it one small step at a time
6. **Iterate** — show to a trusted peer, start draft two, or go back for more references; warns against infinite drafting
7. **Share** — at some point, you decide to put it out

**On finishing:**

- _"There's no end, only beginnings"_ — every project is research for the next one
- Spends time weekly reviewing everything saved to Sublyme — meditative, not a "read later" pile
- Avoids being precious about references; trusts that things appear when they're supposed to

---

### Key Takeaways

- **Intention ≠ goal** — process-orientation sustains creative longevity; outcome-orientation is fragile
- **Your system must fit your life**, not the other way around — borrow frameworks but make them yours
- The two things a system cannot do: discover your intentions for you, or create the work for you
- _"Whatever works for you is what works"_ — no YouTube video can hand you the answer; you already have what you need to start
