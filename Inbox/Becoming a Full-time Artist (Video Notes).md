## Background For Video & Project
- (Rachel) Lifelong dream of being an artist, always chose the "safe" career path instead
- Series of layoffs and a miserable job shifted her perception of what "safe" actually means
### Original plan: 
- Stay at job, save money, give herself one year to build art into a sustainable business with clear revenue benchmarks
- Built a full business plan, marketing plan, and monthly goal checklist

> [!example] ### Quit her job in December 2024
> - Before the challenge officially began — art became her sole income immediately

## Key Take-aways
### What She Made (Collections & Products)
- **Fable Collection** — Victorian storybook-inspired pen & ink and watercolour prints, plus apparel and homewares; hard to market due to a niche audience
- **Pet Portrait Commissions** — Started from a personal Delftware-style painting of her dog that unexpectedly took off on Etsy; expanded into multiple styles and a dedicated Jack Russell Terrier Instagram
- **Postcards from Cincinnati** — Architectural pen & ink drawings of local landmarks (Music Hall, American Sign Museum); a way to explore her new city
- **Misc prints** — Animal idiom series (Catnap, Rat Race), Green Velvet themed products

**Total inventory:** 15 prints, 13 clothing items, 7 other products, 1 architectural commission offering, 10 pet portrait offerings

### Performance Metrics
##### **YouTube**
- 117K views, ~9K watch hours, 4,447 subscribers, $325 ad revenue, ~2M impressions
- First video in the series: 58K views, 2,268 subscribers attributed directly to it
- Key insight: long-form content has lasting shelf life vs. social posts buried in a day
##### **Instagram**
- 15K views, 881 likes, 14 new followers, 83 website visits, 56 Etsy visits
- Modest numbers — treated more as a portfolio than a sales driver
##### **Pinterest** _(discovered mid-year it was auto-posting)_
- 137K impressions, 6K engagements, 133 Etsy visits
- Two top-performing pins drove the majority of results
##### **Email**
- 6–9 clicks per campaign; low traffic driver but high relationship value
##### **Etsy**
- Traffic tripled year-over-year: 11K visits, 1,523 favourites, 138 new followers, 29 five-star reviews, 4 repeat buyers
- 57% of traffic from Etsy's own algorithm/SEO; 43% from Pinterest, Instagram, and paid Etsy ads
###### **Website**
- Only 997 sessions total; YouTube was the top referrer
- Most-clicked FAQ: _"Are your prints your own original art?"_ — 58 unique clicks, reflecting broader concern about AI-generated art

### Goals vs. Benchmarks
- **Goals** = actions within her control (posting, creating, outreach)
- **Benchmarks** = outcomes not fully in her control (commission volume, sales targets, YouTube monetisation)

Best months: **February & March** — completed 9/10 goals AND ranked highest in sales  
Worst month: **April** — returned to full-time work, completed only 2/10 goals

Key finding: a graph of goals completed vs. sales rank showed a clear direct correlation — effort genuinely drove results (with expected holiday spikes as outliers)

#### **Benchmark highlights:**
- Hit YouTube monetisation criteria in just one month ✅
- Reached 10 commission orders in February ✅
- Sold art in 3 in-person locations ✅
- Never consistently hit 30 Etsy sales/month ❌

### What Worked & What Didn't

#### **What worked:**
- **Delftware pet portrait listing** — organically took off with no promotion; drove 64–66% of all sales; acknowledged as partly luck + the right listing catching the algorithm
- **YouTube** — long shelf life, genuine connection, drove meaningful traffic; also partly attributed to luck catching on after a slow start
- **Just showing up** — across all months, simply being active and engaged correlated with better sales regardless of which specific platform or tactic

#### **What didn't work / hurt the business:**
- Website — essentially redundant next to Etsy
- Social media — minimal sales impact; inconsistent use
- Jack Russell Terrier niche account — no traction
- **Doing too much at once** — constantly launching new collections/accounts/strategies led to burnout and overwhelm; hard to maintain multiple spinning plates

**Biggest lesson:** The two biggest wins (the Delft painting and the first YouTube video) were things done with no strategy or expectation. Effort mattered, but not in the neat, controllable way she'd planned for.

### Going Back to Work & Reframing the Dream
- Returned to full-time employment in April — difficult but ultimately the right call
- Discovered full-time artistry wasn't what she'd imagined: _"the grass is plenty green where I'm at"_
- No longer feels the pull of "what if" — the experience gave her clarity and perspective

- **Scrapping the structured 2026 plan** — getting married, two surgeries, still working full-time; not the year for a rigid second full-time commitment
- Pushing the big collection/release plan to **2027**
- 2026 goal: **have fun with art again** — explore new mediums, try new things, no pressure on deadlines or revenue targets
- Will still post updates at least quarterly
- Open to the possibility that a casual, low-pressure approach might actually suit the business better long-term
