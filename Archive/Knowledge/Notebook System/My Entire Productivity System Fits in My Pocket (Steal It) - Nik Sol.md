> [!info]- ## Description
> 
> Most productivity systems try to solve everything with more apps.

But over time I realized something simple: thinking works better when it starts on paper.

In this video I show the simple 3-item pocket productivity system I use every day — a notebook, a pen, and my phone — and how this small workflow helps me capture ideas, filter information, and avoid turning digital tools into clutter.

Instead of building complicated productivity setups, I rely on a simple analog-digital workflow:

• a notebook as a “second brain”
• a quick daily capture system
• a simple evening review
• moving only the valuable ideas into digital tools

You’ll see how I combine paper with digital tools like Notion for organizing books and video ideas, and Taste for saving films to watch later.

This system helps me:

• reduce phone usage
• avoid digital clutter
• capture ideas faster
• stay focused without complex productivity apps

If you're interested in digital minimalism, simple productivity systems, analog note-taking, or building a second brain without overwhelm, this workflow might help you simplify your thinking process.
