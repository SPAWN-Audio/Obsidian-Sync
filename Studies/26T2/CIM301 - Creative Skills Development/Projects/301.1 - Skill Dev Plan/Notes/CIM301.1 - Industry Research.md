## Overview
The landscape of professional audio work is definitely looking at a big shift. Driven by the integration of AI into everything & the increasing demand & implementation of Spatial & 3D Audio. 

With the continued expansion of the Games Industry, smaller games & independent studio's are now filling the gap that larger established studio's have created. As stated in [How Indie games thrive amid AAA stagnation](https://allcorrectgames.com/insights/how-indie-games-thrived-amid-aaa-stagnation/#:~:text=Today%2C%20monetization%20isn't%20about,%2C%20Hollow%20Knight%2C%20or%20Mouthwashing) larger studio's are pushing for more aggressive monetisation schemes eroding public sentiment, where Indie Games are providing players with originality & creativity.

Although this is probably a good move in general for the industry, this does show an emerging theme when it comes to Audio Engineers. 

> *"Generalism with depth wins"*

Clients are no longer just looking for '*a sound guy*' but for someone that can be multi-faceted in their skills. Someone that can wear multiple 'hats' that can deliver high quality work, adapt to multiple toolsets across a range of platforms. 

## Reaper & Pro Tools
Even though Pro Tools might be the de facto tool used in Studio's for recording, mixing & mastering, there's definitely a smaller, quieter revolution towards using Reaper as a main DAW, especially in the Game Audio scene. 

Because Reaper is a lot more customisable, and a lot less resource heavy than Pro Tools - more & more professionals are moving towards Reaper as their main tool. 

Reaper's scripting environment, which supports Lua, Python, and EEL2 allows for automation of repetitive tasks, building custom workflows, and creating tools tailored to specific project requirements. This kind of customisability is increasingly seen as a competitive differentiator, particularly in a market that rewards engineers who can work faster and smarter.

## Middleware Tools
The growing importance of game audio middleware (particularly Wwise & FMOD) needs to be outlined. 
These tools act as the critical bridge between audio assets created in a DAW and the actual game engine itself. Which enables dynamic, adaptive audio systems that respond in real-time to player behaviour, state. 
Having a strong working knowledge of these middleware tools is now widely considered as a must.

## Immersive & Spatial Audio
Spatial & Immersive audio is a rapidly growing segment of the industry, due to advances in technologies & platform expectations. 
Apple Music has made concerted push in spatial audio to differentiate themselves in the market. [^1]
With others like Netflix & Sony strongly encouraging Dolby Atmos mixed content.

With the growing implementation & advancing technologies - this is a big component to professional audio I need to look into learning. 

## AI Tools Revolution
Artificial Intelligence is here to stay wether we like it or not. From audio cleanup tools, to AI stem separation & musical ideation, the audio engineers standing to benefit from this are the ones who can use some of these tools to aid in their workflow.

Although some of these tools might raise the bar for entry level jobs, being able to integrate usable tools, as well as having a general AI literacy can be seen as big boons for recruiters.

[^1]: [Apple Music announces Spatial Audio and Lossless Audio](https://www.apple.com/au/newsroom/2021/05/apple-music-announces-spatial-audio-and-lossless-audio/)
