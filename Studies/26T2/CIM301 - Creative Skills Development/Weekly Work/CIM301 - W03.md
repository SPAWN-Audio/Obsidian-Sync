# Welcome to W03

> [!info] 
> This week you’ll begin shaping the first stage of your project plan by strengthening your understanding of goal-setting frameworks and the structure of deliberate practice. Building on your industry skills analysis, you’ll start aligning your chosen skill with a clear, actionable plan.

## What We'll Cover
Your asynchronous learning introduces SMART goal setting and explores how deliberate practice can be translated into practical project actions. We’ll also look at timelines, planning methods, and the importance of reflective journaling as part of your ongoing development. This is a key point in the trimester, as your project begins to shift from idea to structured plan.

As a reminder, please complete the **Mentor Group Form** if you haven’t already. The link will be available in Slack. Providing this information helps us place you in the most suitable mentor group for next week’s session.

Goal setting and deliberate practice form the backbone of your project work. Establishing a solid plan now will ensure your workload is manageable and your progress is measurable across the trimester. ## Notes
### 3 Components of Skill Development
1. **Reason:** The Skill being developed - *why* you're doing the project
2. **SMARTER Goal Statement:** *What* you're going to do in the project - what you're going to create
3. **Deliberate Practise Project Plan:** *How* you're going to do the project
	- Remember that we want things to be *quantifiable* - It's not so productive to say "My goal is to improve my skill in X"
	- Your **Goal** is to create x number of creative assets - to do x number of focused hours in the studio making them
		- This is all based on the understanding that if you do these things, you will improve your skills
##### So, the structure of what you'll be doing is this:
- "I want to improve my skill in x. (This is the reason for doing what you're about to do.) To do this, I'm going to do set a goal of doing y (This is your SMARTER goal statement)". To achieve this goal, I'm going to z (This is your deliberate practice plan)."
