Your asynchronous session explores deliberate practice — improving a skill by working just beyond your current ability, using focused effort and regular feedback. ## Autodidact
#### What does it mean?
- An autodidact is a self-taught person who acquires knowledge or skills independently, without formal instruction or traditional schooling
#### Why is Important?
- Important to have that skill as creative professional you need to constantly be leveling up your skills to stay both relevant as well as competitive in the industry
#### What makes a competent Autodidact? 
- They need to be able to optimise learning - knowing how to best learn & optimise the system
- They need to be able identify crucial & important information from redundant & irrelevant information
- Need to have good structure & frameworks in place & to be able to organise knowledge properly
- to be able to focus in on tasks & activities that are urgent & important
- Need to know how to utilise deliberate practise to constantly level up skills

### Being an Effective Autodidact
1. Identify **demands** for particular skills (*industry skills analysis*)
2. Assess their own **skill set** & identify areas that need improving (*SWOT Analysis*)
3. Establish an appropriate **GOAL** to develop that skill (*SMARTER Goals*)
4. Build an effective **project plan** to achieve that goal (Deliberate Practise) 
5. Carry out & **preserve** with the project (*GRIT*)

## SMARTER Goals 

> The SMARTER goal framework can be used to construct a clear and effective goal statement. This goal statement will form the basis of a project.

1. **Specific** - The goal should be well-defined and clear, focusing on a specific area or objective.
2. **Measurable** - There must be criteria for measuring progress and determining when the goal has been achieved. When 'x' is achieved, the project is done.
3. **Achievable** - The goal should be realistic and attainable, considering the resources and constraints at hand.
4. **Relevant** - The goal should align with broader objectives or long-term ambitions, making sure it is important and worthwhile.
5. **Time-bound** - A clear deadline or timeframe should be established to ensure the goal is time-sensitive and prioritised.
6. **Evaluated** - Performance and progress towards the goal is continually assessed.
7. **Revised** - The goal is adjusted as needed based on feedback or changing circumstances.
