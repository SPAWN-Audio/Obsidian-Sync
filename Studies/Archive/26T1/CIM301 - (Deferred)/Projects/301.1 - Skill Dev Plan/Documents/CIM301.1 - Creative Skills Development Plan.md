# Industry Skills Research

^f60b3f

> [!example] 500 Words Minimum
> ### Industry Skills Research
> 
> > [!info]-
> > Research your industry and identify current or emerging skills that you are interested in. Focus on the areas that align with your interests and professional goals, and note how these skills might influence your future career. Your discussion should be at least 500 words and include at least one APA-cited reference
> 
> ## Overview
> The landscape of professional audio work is definitely looking at a big shift. Driven by the integration of AI into everything & the increasing demand & implementation of Spatial & 3D Audio. 
> 
> With the continued expansion of the Games Industry, smaller games & independent studio's are now filling the gap that larger established studio's have created. As stated in [How Indie games thrive amid AAA stagnation](https://allcorrectgames.com/insights/how-indie-games-thrived-amid-aaa-stagnation/#:~:text=Today%2C%20monetization%20isn't%20about,%2C%20Hollow%20Knight%2C%20or%20Mouthwashing) larger studio's are pushing for more aggressive monetisation schemes eroding public sentiment, where Indie Games are providing players with originality & creativity.
> 
> Although this is probably a good move in general for the industry, this does show an emerging theme when it comes to Audio Engineers. 
> 
> > *"Generalism with depth wins"*
> 
> Clients are no longer just looking for '*a sound guy*' but for someone that can be multi-faceted in their skills. Someone that can wear multiple 'hats' that can deliver high quality work, adapt to multiple toolsets across a range of platforms. 
> 
> ---
> 
> ## Reaper & Pro Tools
> Even though Pro Tools might be the de facto tool used in Studio's for recording, mixing & mastering, there's definitely a smaller, quieter revolution towards using Reaper as a main DAW, especially in the Game Audio scene. 
> 
> Because Reaper is a lot more customisable, and a lot less resource heavy than Pro Tools - more & more professionals are moving towards Reaper as their main tool. 
> 
> Reaper's scripting environment, which supports Lua, Python, and EEL2 allows for automation of repetitive tasks, building custom workflows, and creating tools tailored to specific project requirements. This kind of customisability is increasingly seen as a competitive differentiator, particularly in a market that rewards engineers who can work faster and smarter.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## Middleware Tools
> The growing importance of game audio middleware (particularly Wwise & FMOD) needs to be outlined. 
> These tools act as the critical bridge between audio assets created in a DAW and the actual game engine itself. Which enables dynamic, adaptive audio systems that respond in real-time to player behaviour, state. 
> Having a strong working knowledge of these middleware tools is now widely considered as a must.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## Immersive & Spatial Audio
> Spatial & Immersive audio is a rapidly growing segment of the industry, due to advances in technologies & platform expectations. 
> Apple Music has made concerted push in spatial audio to differentiate themselves in the market. [^1]
> With others like Netflix & Sony strongly encouraging Dolby Atmos mixed content.
> 
> With the growing implementation & advancing technologies - this is a big component to professional audio I need to look into learning. 
> 
> ---
> 
> ## AI Tools Revolution
> Artificial Intelligence is here to stay wether we like it or not. From audio cleanup tools, to AI stem separation & musical ideation, the audio engineers standing to benefit from this are the ones who can use some of these tools to aid in their workflow.
> 
> Although some of these tools might raise the bar for entry level jobs, being able to integrate usable tools, as well as having a general AI literacy can be seen as big boons for recruiters.

# Industry Skills Gap Analysis (SWOT)

> [!example] 500 Words Minimum
> ### Personal SWOT Analysis 
> 
> > [!info]-
> > Assess your current skill set by comparing it with the skills required in your industry. Use a SWOT analysis to explore your Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats, focusing on both hard skills (technical abilities) and soft skills (interpersonal skills). Your reflection should be at least 500 words.
> 
> ## Strengths
> My strongest pillars across my audio skillset definitely resides in the realms of Sound Design, Foley & Music Production. Ableton is definitely my strongest tool, especially for quick ideation & music production, although for larger more complex projects, especially for Screen, Pro Tools is the tool I'd use & something I'd consider quite competent in. 
> 
> Alongside these main tools, I'd consider myself competent with Wwise for implementation of audio into games, as well as iZotope RX for advanced audio repair, utilising this heavily on previous film projects to cleanup dialogue & recordings. For Music specifically I'm quite well versed with iZotope Ozone to polish musical masters ready for release. 
> 
> Outside of my software hard skills, I've received positive feedback for the work I've done with Location sound with both sound mixing & boom operation, but more importantly a lot of the feedback on set has been on how I work well with the team, that I bring a fun but productive air to the way that I work. 
> 
> Continuing on with my Soft Skills, I have a strong project management methodologies, with my Capstone being the one to initiate a collaborative mindset to the project, leading to our group juggling 4 different Capstone projects concurrently. I also have strong interpersonal skills built from years of customer facing jobs in hospitality. I think these alone would be highly beneficial to freelance work, as well as for work at Studio's & Production companies. 
> 
> ---
> 
> ## Weaknesses 
> FMOD is another highly used middleware tool for game studios, as well as this more studio's especially smaller ones highly favour audio people with skills & knowledge in scripting & coding, with C#, Python & Lua being the most common cited [^2]
> 
> Reaper is a DAW thats growing in popularity especially in Game Audio, as it offers a lot of functionality that other DAW's don't have, and a scripting tool / function that makes it completely customisable which puts it into a class of it's own. With it's growing popularity I really need to put in some time to learn how to use it, as well as the capabilities & workflows.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## Opportunities 
> Opportunities might seem a little sparse initially but expanding the scope to smaller pockets of audio work definitely makes things look a little better. 
> 
> With the growing markets of Audiobooks, as well as Podcasts & YouTubers opens up a lot of possible work & pathways. This can be for both Sound Design & Music.
> 
> Spatial & 3D Audio are also niches in audio that are currently taking off, with projections looking at roughly $6.5 billion in 2024 jumping to estimates of $19.2 billion by 2034 according to [3D Audio Market](https://market.us/report/3d-audio-market/) [^3]
> 
> Outside of traditional work opportunities, there's definitely a growing market to freelance tutoring for tools like Ableton. As shown in [^3] teens outline that they're looking for more variety in the way that they learn music.
> 
> ---
> 
> ## Threats
> Perth definitely has a limited market when it comes to certain pockets of audio work - especially Games Audio. Though remote work is possible, it has been stated that studios often prefer local talent as team integration & time zones can at times provide issues.  
> 
> Although there's some possible future work in Film Audio with the creation of [Perth Film Studios](https://perthfilmstudios.com) some say that directors, producers, etc already have their established network, so for a newcomer into the scene, it'll be a lot harder to break into some of these roles. 
> 
> AI is definitely a possible threat, even though they won't (or currently unable) to take the higher creative work, it's definitely at a place where it can replace the smaller, entry level tasks & roles. 
> 
> *For Example:*
> - Basic Editing tasks - cutting out silences between takes 
> - Ideation & basic - coming up with small, quick ideas
