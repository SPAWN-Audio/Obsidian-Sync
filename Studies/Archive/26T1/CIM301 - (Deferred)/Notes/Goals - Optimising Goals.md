## Know the resources required for reaching Goals
Personal desire for reaching a goal is powerful, but often other resources are needed as well. What resources do you need to reach your goals - time, money, position? Additional education? The help of a particular person? 

*For example:* 
Consider the goal of purchasing a computer system for your home office. The resources you might need include: money to purchase the computer, the software, and the printer; additional knowledge for knowing how to use it; and the help of a computer consultant.

### Strategies for Staying Power
The statements to which you responded relate to strategies that encourage your “staying power” when working toward a goal. Most of us benefit from strategies that strengthen our resolve. Each of these strategies is discussed in detail in this chapter. As you read, note your areas of strength as well as opportunities for improvement

## Work with Goals that are Compatible
When goals conflict with one another they compete for your time, energy, and personal resources, which can leave you feeling drained and stressed. 

#### *For example:* 
A manager was challenged by his boss to spend more time on the production floor interacting with his employees, and then was criticised for not spending enough time at his desk. To him, these two goals were in conflict with one another. Another example is the manager who wanted to complete her doctorate. She enrolled at a university and was beginning her studies when she learned that she was pregnant. 

In analysing the situation, she discovered that the goals of pursuing an education, maintaining a full-time job, and starting a family were not compatible at that time. Trying to meet the challenges of these goals would deplete her resources of energy, time, and enthusiasm. 

Review your goals. Which ones are competing for your resources? How can you modify or change your goals so that they are more compatible?

## Accept the Responsibility for doing the work that is necessary to reach your Goals
Establishing goals is a start. 
Accepting the responsibility to accomplish them is another challenge. When the going gets tough, many people begin to blame others for their lack of progress. 

Instead of getting caught in a defensive, blaming trap, apply your time and energy to solving problems, making decisions, and working your plan so that your goals are realised.

> [!example]- #### How do you typically react when faced with a problem? 
> - Depending on the situation I think I react alright to problems, though after prolonged extents in high stress projects, etc I think I can maybe get a little annoyed, etc. 

> [!example]- #### Does your reaction get in the way of your potential success?
> - No I don't think so for the most part

> [!example]- #### Are you willing to change your behaviour? If so, how?
> - Yes, making sure that I maintain a Growth Mindset at all times, and always to see challenges & problems as potential ways of furthering my skills & self development


## Minimise the interruptions that block your progress
Many interruptions have the appearance of urgency and importance, but often they are time wasters and trivial matters. When an interruption arises, quickly evaluate it in terms of its importance to your goals. If it is not an enabler to your progress, then it may be a hindrance.

> [!example]- #### Do you control interruptions or do interruptions control you?
> - I think that sometimes I can let interruptions take control of what I'm doing, and if not that exactly, I think that it can definitely de-rail me significantly

> [!example]- #### Do you handle interruptions swiftly and decisively?
> - No not at all, I sometimes get sucked into the interruptions & end up wasting a lot of time 

> [!example]- #### What specific actions can you take to minimise the interruptions to your day?
> - I can make specific decisions to work in places that minimise distractions & interruptions out of my control, *ie:* going into the studio at SAE instead of studying at home where I can sometimes get distracted by my roommates, etc. 
> - I can setup certain things that automatically minimise things that can possibly distract / interrupt me *ie:* phone & notifications, etc  


## Be flexible to changes that affect your progress toward a Goal
Although a planned path optimises progress toward your goals, the unexpected does sometimes arise and can either impede or facilitate progress. When an unforeseen change in your plan occurs, evaluate it for damage control. Brainstorm alternatives to eliminate or minimise the impact of the problem, but also look for opportunities!

An advertising agency that was growing quickly felt several pressures affecting its progress toward its five-year goal of becoming known nationally for creative thinking. The company’s president died unexpectedly, a multimillion-dollar account was lost, the economy forced several other clients to drop the services, and several key management people left the company. 
Instead of allowing these changes to debilitate the company, agency executives sought opportunities in the challenges. They streamlined the agency, recommitted themselves to a vision of what the agency could be, and sought outside assistance to strengthen skills.

> [!example]- #### What are the potential problems or changes that could get in the way of reaching one of your goals?
> ##### The current state of the Industry - the over saturation of audio practitioners & the low state of the economy
> - *Doesn't help breaking into the industry any easier, so will have to strategise properly & to work hard*
> ##### My current toxic habits - both work, sleep & personal habits
> - *I will have break these bad / toxic habits if I will actually forge a strong & successful career* 

> [!example]- #### What actions will you take to optimise your opportunities and minimise potential problems?
> ##### Action to Optimise
> - Personal Routines & Structures
> - Strategise a proper plan of attack 
> - Build *good* habits that will help me to maintain a strong work ethic
> 	- Gym & Exercise routine 
> 	- Good working habits
> ##### Action to Minimise
> - Bad Habits & routines 
> 	- Breaking bad sleep habits
> 	- Breaking bad work habits & mentalities 


## Identify the Benefits of Reaching your Goals
Get tuned into WII-FM (What’s in It for Me?). Knowing and wanting the goal’s benefits is a key factor in motivating you to apply your time, energy, and resources. 
No salesperson is successful without an effort to sell the benefits of a product or a service to the client. So sell your goal’s benefits to yourself. 
Perhaps the benefits include exposure to upper management, a chance to learn something new, or the opportunity for a promotion. When you value the benefit that you’ll receive, that value increases your motivation to reach the goal.

> [!example]- #### What factors motivate you to work toward a particular goal?
> - Creating a long lasting successful career
> - Financial Freedom
> - Work & Career satisfaction

> [!example]- #### What are the benefits of reaching one or more of your goals?
> - A step closer to some of the things outlined above
> - Solidifying some better habits & fundamental skills
> - Gaining practise & experience within the industry

## Review your progress toward your Goals regularly
Reviewing your goals not only identifies the progress you’ve made, but also provides an opportunity to ensure that you are on track and that you have adequate resources at your disposal. 

A review of your goals also fans the fire of motivation by highlighting your accomplishments. Consider posting a list of your goals so you can see them on a daily basis. Charting your progress regularly is also motivating.

> [!example]- #### Do you make a point of reviewing your goals on a regular basis?
> - I don't currently but know that this is very important to furthering my goals
> - I need to make sure that I set this up & continue to use this. 

> [!example]- #### Would charting your progress increase your motivation?
> - Yes definitely - I have already downloaded some Obsidian Plugins to help chart this. Need to think about the best & easiest way to do this, to ensure that I build the habit as well as maintain it. 


## Balance your efforts among the Goals that are important to you
Often we find ourselves applying a disproportionate amount of time to certain goals. One successful realtor who sold 7 million dollars’ worth of real estate in a year realised that while she said that her family was very important to her, in reality she did not share much of her time and energy with them. Instead, most of her resources went to her job. Those long hours might ensure job performance, but goals related to other areas of life (family, friends, community, or spiritual life) may suffer.

> [!example]- #### In Chapter 1 you identified goals related to different areas of your life. Do you apply adequate amounts of time, energy, and resources to each of those areas that are important to you? If not, what changes are you willing to make?
> - No - I'll make sure to check in with myself & to aim at building a better balance between all of the different areas 


## Reach your Goals by concentrating on those things that relate to your Goals
Whatever activities are planned in your day, consider their value to reaching your goals. Frequently, there are activities that you can eliminate or modify so that you can concentrate on making progress toward your significant goals.

> [!example]- #### Are you willing to give priority to those activities that contribute specifically to your goals? How can you do this?
> - I need to consistently check in on the tasks that I work on - making sure that they're relevant & help me step closer towards my goals
> - Go through the action plans & steps for my goals - prioritise things better 

Experiencing progress toward goals that are meaningful to you is gratifying and can motivate you toward even more accomplishments. Using these eleven strategies nearly guarantees progress toward reaching your goals. Prepare to reap the benefits of your effort!
