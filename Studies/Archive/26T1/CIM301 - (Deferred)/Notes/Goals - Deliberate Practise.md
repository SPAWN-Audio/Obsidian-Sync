# Deliberate Practise 1

Deliberate practice is a structured and purposeful approach to improving a skill. 
It involves focused, consistent practice with the goal of mastering specific aspects of a skill. These 'specific aspects' of the skill are those that need the most improvement. Key characteristics of deliberate practice include:

- **Goal Orientated:** Each session targets a specific skill or weakness
- **Repetitive:** Skills are refined through repeated, focused effort
- **Challenging:** Tasks push beyond current abilities to encourage growth
- **Feedback Driven:** Regular feedback is essential to identify mistakes & guide improvement
- **Reflection:** The Project Plan is periodically evaluated & adjusted based on reflections on progress

Unlike regular practice, deliberate practice is highly intentional, aiming for gradual and sustained improvement through focused effort.

## Creating a Deliberate Practise Plan
> You want to make sure your sessions to be focussed and intentional. To ensure this, provide a plan as to...

### How the sessions are going to Goal Orientated 
- Make a list of the individual aspects of the activity you believe will be most important to develop.
- Break down the project into manageable chunks and milestones.
- In each session, plan exactly what you're going to be working on.

### How you will ensure that each session is Challenging
- What are some techniques that you really want to, or will need to, get better at to achieve the goal of the project?

### How you will be incorporating *repetition* into the sessions
- Ensure your project plan repeating, cyclical processes, rather than only a linear one.
	- For example, say your project was about Lyric writing. You might spend your first week focussing solely practicing rhyming, with a 1 hour session each day dedicated to this. Then, in week 2, you might spend a week solely exploring different narrative structures (while maintaining the rhyming techniques). Then in week 3, you might explore something else... The idea is that the project is carried out through blocks of repetitive practice sessions.
- Skills are refined through repeated, focused effort

### How you will seek & receive *feedback*
- Who will you seek feedback from?
- How will receive this feedback?
- What will you do with this feedback?

### How you will incorporate *reflection* into the practise
- Where will you carry out these reflections?
