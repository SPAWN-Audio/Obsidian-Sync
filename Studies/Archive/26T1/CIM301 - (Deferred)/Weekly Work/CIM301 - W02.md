> [!info] 
> This week shifts attention to understanding your current strengths, weaknesses, and opportunities within the creative industries. By analysing your skills profile, you’ll begin clarifying which area of development will be most meaningful for your project.

## What We'll Cover
Your asynchronous activities focus on industry skills analysis, identifying personal skills gaps, and using tools like SWOT analysis to position your goals. This helps ensure that your chosen skill is not only interesting to you, but strategically relevant to your future pathways.

You’ll also be introduced to the **Mentor Group Form**, which will be sent to you via Slack. This short Google Form lets the teaching team know which project and skill area you’re considering, so we can place you in an appropriate mentor group.

## Why This Matters
A clear understanding of your skill profile allows you to design a more targeted and effective project. Completing the Mentor Group Form early helps ensure you are supported by the right mentor from Week 4 onward, giving you a smoother path through the unit.

### Horizons of Focus
- [The 6 Horizons of Focus](https://gettingthingsdone.com/2011/01/the-6-horizons-of-focus/)

David Allen's "Horizons of Focus" is a framework from his Getting Things Done (GTD) methodology. 
It helps individuals organise their tasks and goals across different levels of perspective. It consists of six "horizons", each representing a progressively broader view of one's life and priorities:

- **Ground Level** - Day to day actions - current tasks & todo lists
- **Horizon 1** - Projects - short term goals that require multiple steps to complete
- **Horizon 2** - Areas of responsibility - key roles & ongoing commitments, such as a job, study or family
- **Horizon 3** - Goals - short to medium-term objectives (1-2 years)
- **Horizon 4** - Vision - Long-term aspirations (3-5 years)
- **Horizon 5** - Purpose & Values - the fundamental reasons for actions, guiding principles, & life mission. *The Why* - Why are you doing any of this?

Each horizon helps clarify actions, align tasks with higher goals, and ensure that daily activities contribute to long-term success.
