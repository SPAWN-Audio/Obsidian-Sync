# Unit Overview

The transition from study to work is an important step for you as an emerging professional in the creative industries. 
Work Integrated Learning provides you with a professional experience opportunity that both complements and further deepens the skills and knowledge you have gained from study. 

Throughout your studies you have had to learn on the job (in projects), build strong relationships with people, develop strong communication skills, and use a range of transferable skills. 
During your work placement you will put this knowledge and skill set into action in a real- world professional workplace. This is a valuable opportunity to gain experience in a professional setting, and further develop your job-readiness. The Work Placement requires a minimum of 80 hours at one or more host organisations. 

Both the student and the host are required to complete an SAE Agreement that formalises the placement. Work placements can only commence after Stage 1 of your Bachelor course has been completed, and only upon successful completion of CIE152 and approval from the local Industry Learning Partner (ILP). If approved by your ILP, you may undertake a work placement before official enrolment in CIM310, and ‘bank’ the work placement hours.

# Assessment 1: Job Application Assets

| TITLE                           | NOTE                                                          |
| ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Group / Individual Project**  | Individual                                                    |
| **Artificial Intelligence Use** | Level 2: possible AI-assisted idea generation and structuring |

## Overview
You will attend work placement seminars and activities that will help you prepare for your work placement and future employment. Activities will include:

1. **Reviewing your current work placement application materials**
	- **Portfolio:** The purpose of a portfolio is primarily to demonstrate the highlights of your academic and creative abilities or skill sets. This collection of works should be engaging, creative, and professionally presented and will be an essential element in your transition from student to emerging professional.
	- **Resume:** A well-structured one-page resume will showcase your skills, document your experience, link to your e-portfolio and provide some information about your goals. Your resume will also include your contact details.
	- **Cover Letter:** A professional cover letter is tailored to the opportunity you are applying for and should respond to the selection criteria and position description. Your cover letter is an opportunity to showcase your skills, experience and knowledge and how they apply to the position.
2. Participation in the work placement seminars and scheduled classes will also provide you with an overview of the professional and cultural expectations of work placement and an understanding of key legislative issues, namely, Occupational Health & Safety (OHS), discrimination, sexual harassment, etc.
#### IMPORTANT
You should complete 1 and 2 above before undertaking a work placement, as it supports your understanding of professional, cultural, and legislative responsibilities essential for a safe and successful placement experience. 

You are required to dedicate time to researching the industry and suitable host organisations. You can then apply for potential work placement opportunities. Where possible, you should apply directly to the company under the advice of the Industry Learning Partner (ILP). The ILP will assist you if you are unsuccessful in obtaining a position yourself. It is expected, as an emerging professional, that you will make a genuine attempt at finding a placement first.

## Steps to Follow:
#### Attend Work Placement Classes
1. Participate in preparatory classes (Weeks 1 - 3) and activities. 
2. Engage with sessions on professional and cultural expectations in the workplace, which may include training on Occupational Health & Safety (OHS), discrimination, and sexual harassment.
#### Prepare Application Classes
1. **Portfolio:** Select and compile highlights of your academic and creative work. Ensure presentation is professional, engaging, and relevant to your desired industry. It has to be published online and public-facing
2. **Resume:** Develop a concise, one-page resume that includes contact details, skills, experience, goals, and a link to your e-portfolio
3. **Cover Letter:** Draft a tailored cover letter responding directly to a position description/selection criteria, demonstrating transferable and discipline-specific skills.
#### Seek Work Placement Opportunities
1. Research the industry and identify potential host organisations. 
2. Apply directly to organisations where possible, using your resume, cover letter, and portfolio. 
3. Seek support from your Industry Learning Partner (ILP) only if self- sourced applications are unsuccessful.
#### Gain Approval for Placement
1. Ensure attendance of seminars, classes, and submission of application materials before placement is approved

### Deliverables: What to Submit
- **Portfolio:** Professionally presented web-published showcase of academic/creative works and skill sets. 
- **Resume:** One-page professional document (.pdf) including your skills, experience, goals, contact details, and e-portfolio link. 
- **Cover Letter:** Document (.pdf) tailored to a specific placement opportunity/s with clear response to selection criteria/position description.

# Assessment 2: Evidence of Work

| ------------------------------- | -------------------- |
| **Group / Individual Project**  | Individual           |
| **Artificial Intelligence Use** | Level 1: No AI usage |

## Overview
This assessment focuses on the comprehensive documentation of your placement experience through tangible work evidence and verified timesheet records. By creating authentic Evidence of Work documentation, you demonstrate workplace learning and professional development while also providing clear verification of placement completion. 

As a stage 3 unit, the assessment consolidates skills developed in earlier study and supports both your professional growth and readiness for future employment. Students are required to have their timesheets recorded weekly by the host organisation, showing no less than 80 hours by the end of placement. 

It is the students' responsibility to submit their signed timesheet as part of their 'Evidence of Work' Project

## Project Goals: What to Create 
#### The goal of this project is to: 
- Demonstrate authentic workplace engagement through tangible evidence of contributions 
- Document professional development and skill application in real workplace contexts
- Maintain accurate records of placement hours with appropriate supervisory verification
- Showcase ability to communicate workplace learning effectively
- Provide evidence of problem-solving approaches employed during placement

## Steps to Follow
#### Documentation Preparation: 
- Throughout your placement, collect work samples, contribution evidence, and maintain daily timesheet records using the timesheet template provided with supervisory signature 
#### Portfolio Compilation: 
- Organise work evidence with reflective commentary explaining relevance to unit learning outcomes 
#### Timesheet Verification: 
- Ensure all placement hours are accurately logged with a supervisory verification signature 
#### Professional Presentation: 
- Present portfolio materials in a clear, professional format, demonstrating effective communication skills 
#### Submission: 
- Submit a complete portfolio and verified timesheet documentation via Campus Online by the end of Week 12

### Deliverables: What to Submit
1. Portfolio of Work Evidence: Comprehensive compilation of work samples, contributions, and achievements from placement experience (.pdf) 
2. Verified Timesheet Documentation: Detailed log of all placement hours with supervisory validation. (.pdf)

| ------------------------------- | ---------- |
| **Group / Individual Project**  | Individual |
| **Artificial Intelligence Use** | N/A        |

## Overview
The host supervisor will assess student skills and overall performance.

Your professional work placement behaviour and practices will be assessed by your work placement supervisor using the host assessment document which is aligned to the Transferable Skills Framework.

The host assessment takes into consideration:

- Ability to follow instructions
- Communication skills 
- Adherence to the host company's code of conduct 
- Display of initiative.

The host will also be asked to highlight areas in which the student excelled, as well as those needing improvement.

The ILP will communicate with the host organisation and collect assessment on the student’s behalf. The completed host assessment will be uploaded to Campus Online when complete.
