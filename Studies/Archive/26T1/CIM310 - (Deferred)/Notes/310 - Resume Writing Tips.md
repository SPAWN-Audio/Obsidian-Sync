# Resume Writing Tips

1. Your resume provides a professional summary about you. It's a chronological journey of your education, employment, skillset and software capabilities.
2. It needs to attract attention to get you to the interview stage and stand out from the rest. Make sure it is formatted clearly and easy to read.
3. File name etiquette is important. Eg. Resume of First name Last name (Resume of Daisy Jones) Demonstrate a concise and organised manner.
4. Keep it succinct - a student resume is only one page, while an experienced professional is approx two.
5. Your resume is one of the most strategic marketing documents in your toolbox - don't waste it!

## A Bad Resume

- Is disorganised and difficult to read
- Has long chunks of text instead of bullet points
- Includes an unprofessional email address
- Has spelling and grammar mistakes
- It is not tailored to the job position
- Is not matched with a cover letter
- Includes distracting graphics
- Is not tailored to the position
