# 🎧 Me & Music Journey

---

My musical journey has been a big part of what has shaped my musical tastes as well as my musical sound now. From beginnings influences from my parents, listening to the likes of *Cream, Led Zeppelin,* and *The Beatles*. To finding my own interests in new styles of Hip-Hop & Rap emerging at the time (early 2000's). 

A big pivot in my musical tastes came with an unexpected gift from my mum in 2003, with *Linkin Parks - Meteora.* This opened up a whole new avenue of music for me, diving into Heavy Metal & other adjacent sub-genres. 

From this I began learning the drums, from which I joined the high school Jazz band & Concert band, as a percussionist, as well as being part of the schools first 'Rock Band' - writing and recording an original track, and performing it against other high-schoolers in a 'Battle of the Bands' competition.

From inspirations of recording the original track, spawned ideas of being able to make music digitally. After finding D.A.W's and… *certain* ways of obtaining the expensive applications (on a very slim student budget) I began teaching myself how to use it.

### DJ-ing & Performance 

While learning how to produce I fell in love with a lot of EDM music, from House to Drum and Bass and even Dubstep. This led to me and my 3x house mates at the time splitting the costs of some DJ equipment, we learnt how to play together and started booking local events to perform. 

This transitioned into me playing out at different nightclubs every Friday & Saturday night. I slowly stopped DJ-ing to put more of a focus on my own music and possible career pathways I can make through it.

## What makes my Music Unique 

I believe what makes my music unique is my style, which often blends the elements of different genres together creating something that sounds both familiar and fresh. From smashing together heavy crunchy drums with soft synths and deep rolling basslines, to using the sounds of Dubsteps deep, heavy synth basses with chopping hiphop drums, all knocking at a tempo slightly outside either genres. 

Another unique element I think helps my musical style stand out is how I take one musical idea and morph / transform it into different ideas, changing genres & styles all within the same track.

## Remixes & Bootlegs

The basic strategy for the initial launch (musically) is to focus on using *Remixes & Bootlegs ('Flips')* to first get some music out there, as it's been shown that remixes be great at expanding the reach via complementary audiences.

I plan to also tie these remixes / release them on significant dates *ie:* anniversary of the album, artist birthdays, death anniversaries, etc. Using the hype around the date to further push the remix out there.

Not only this, but Remixes & Bootlegs are often sought after for DJ's as they offer a fun way to involve commonly / recognisable music when they perform, while also not straying from the genres and styles that they need to perform within.

> * Start Small and Build: Don't feel pressured to have a full album ready for your first release. A well-promoted single is a great way to test the waters and build initial traction.

> * Don't get discouraged by slow progress.
> * Seek Feedback: Share your music with trusted sources and be open to constructive criticism.
