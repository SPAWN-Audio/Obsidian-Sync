# Brief 

> [!example] #### Goal 1 - 🏋️‍♂️ Return to Bodybuilding
> - By December 2026, I will return to the gym and build up to a consistent 3-day per week training schedule — following a structured beginner-to-intermediate programme for someone returning after a break — tracking workouts in a simple log for at least 40 out of 52 weeks.

- **ADHD adjustment:** The biggest mistake returning to training after a break is going too hard too fast — the excitement spike is classic ADHD, followed by burnout and quitting. This goal is deliberately paced. 3 days is the ceiling, not the floor. Start at 2 if needed.
- **Visualise it:** Picture yourself 6 months in — back in a rhythm, noticing your strength returning, clothes fitting differently, walking into the gym like you belong there again. That feeling of reclaiming something you lost.
- **Manageable steps:**
    - Month 1–2: 2 sessions per week, full body, moderate weight — reintroduce movement without destroying yourself
    - Month 3–4: Add the 3rd session, introduce a push/pull/legs or upper/lower split
    - Month 5–8: Focus on progressive overload — small, consistent weight increases each week
    - Month 9–12: Reassess and set a specific physique or strength goal for the next 12 months
- **Potential problem:** Comparing current self to past self and feeling discouraged — your previous training means muscle memory is real. Strength and size will return faster than it did the first time. Trust the process.
- **Reward:** After 8 consecutive weeks of consistent training, invest in something training-related that excites you — new gym kit, a programme from a coach you admire, a supplement you've been considering.
