# Brief 

> [!example] #### Goal 3 - 🛌 Fix Sleep & Bedtime Routine
> - By December 2026, I will establish a consistent sleep routine — aiming for 7–8 hours per night with a regular wind-down habit starting 30 minutes before bed — tracked at least 5 nights per week, with a target of being in bed by a set time at least 4 nights per week.

- **ADHD adjustment:** Sleep is where ADHD, low mood, burnout, and poor training recovery all collide. ADHD brains resist sleep because nighttime is often when the brain finally feels quiet and stimulated — leading to "revenge bedtime procrastination." This goal doesn't demand perfection, just a consistent anchor bedtime and a wind-down that signals safety to your nervous system.
- **Visualise it:** Picture waking up before your alarm, actually feeling rested — not dragging yourself out of bed into a foggy, heavy day. That quality of rest changes everything: mood, training performance, stress tolerance, and motivation all improve downstream.
- **Manageable steps:**
    - Month 1: Track your current sleep for 2 weeks without changing anything — just observe patterns honestly
    - Month 2–3: Set one anchor bedtime and one simple wind-down habit (e.g. no screens 30 mins before bed, dim lights, a set playlist or podcast)
    - Month 4–6: Align your training schedule so gym sessions aren't too late in the evening, which disrupts cortisol and delays sleep
    - Month 7–12: Refine based on what's working — consider a sleep tracking app or wearable to identify patterns
- **Potential problem:** Late night hyperfocus (very common with ADHD) blowing past bedtime — set a phone alarm called "START winding down" 45 minutes before your target bedtime. It's not a bedtime alarm, it's a _warning_ alarm. That reframe helps ADHD brains respond better.
- **Reward:** After 4 consistent weeks of hitting your sleep target, treat yourself to something that enhances sleep quality further — a better pillow, a white noise machine, blackout curtains. Investing in the reward reinforces the habit.
