# Brief

> [!example] #### Goal 3 - 📅 Build a Consistent Social Routine
> - By December 2026, I will establish at least one recurring social habit per week — whether a regular check-in with someone, a monthly group activity, or a standing plan with a friend or family member — so that socialising becomes a natural rhythm rather than a one-off effort.

- **ADHD adjustment:** ADHD brains struggle with initiating social contact because it requires executive function every single time — "who do I text, what do I say, when do I go?" A _routine_ removes that decision fatigue. When it's already scheduled and expected, the barrier to showing up drops dramatically.
- **Visualise it:** Picture having a standing Thursday evening call with a mate, or a monthly Sunday lunch with family — something in the calendar that just _happens_, that you don't have to mentally negotiate every week. Social connection that runs on autopilot.
- **Manageable steps:**
    - Month 1–2: Identify one person or group who would suit a recurring, low-pressure social habit
    - Month 3–4: Propose one standing arrangement — keep it simple, same time, same format each week or month
    - Month 5–6: Protect it like an appointment — put it in your calendar with a reminder so ADHD doesn't quietly let it slip
    - Month 7–12: Once the first routine feels natural, consider adding a second one — a group activity or interest-based regular event
- **Potential problem:** The routine breaking after one missed week and never recovering — build in a simple restart rule: one missed week is fine, two in a row means you reschedule immediately, not eventually.
- **Reward:** After 2 months of a consistent social routine, acknowledge it — that's genuinely hard to build and you did it. Mark it somehow, a journal note, a small celebration, something that registers the achievement.
