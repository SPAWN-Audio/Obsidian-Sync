# Brief

> [!example] #### Goal 2 - 💵 Build a Basic Budget
> - By Month 4, I will have a simple working budget in place — tracking all income (including benefits, support, or any freelance work) against all essential outgoings — and will have identified and eliminated at least 3 unnecessary expenses, reviewed weekly every Sunday.

- **ADHD adjustment:** Traditional budgeting systems are too complex for ADHD brains to maintain — spreadsheets get abandoned, apps get ignored, notebooks get lost. This goal uses the simplest possible system: one running total, checked once a week, for 10 minutes. Consistency beats complexity every time.
- **Visualise it:** Picture knowing — every Sunday — exactly where you stand financially that week. Not perfect, not solved, but _known_. That knowledge is power. It stops the spiral of spending without awareness, which is where debt silently grows.
- **Manageable steps:**
    - Month 1: While still building Goal 1's debt list, simultaneously start tracking every penny spent for 30 days — no judgment, just data. Use a free app like Monzo, Emma, or even a Notes app
    - Month 2: Identify your essential outgoings (rent, food, utilities, transport) vs. non-essential (subscriptions, takeaways, impulse purchases)
    - Month 3: Cancel or pause every non-essential subscription and recurring charge you can — these are silent budget killers
    - Month 4: Set a weekly "financial check-in" every Sunday — 10 minutes, check your balance, log spending, adjust if needed
- **Regarding income:** While unemployed, urgently explore all entitled support — Universal Credit, housing benefit, council tax reduction. Contact Citizens Advice (free) if unsure what you're entitled to. Every pound of support claimed is a pound that isn't added to debt.
- **Potential problem:** Impulse buying triggered by low mood, stress, or dopamine-seeking (very common with ADHD and depression) — identify your top 3 impulse triggers and create a specific rule for each. A simple "24 hour rule" — wait 24 hours before any non-essential purchase — can dramatically reduce impulsive spending.
- **Reward:** Every week you complete your Sunday check-in, mark it on a visible calendar. Watching that streak build is genuinely motivating — and it costs nothing.
