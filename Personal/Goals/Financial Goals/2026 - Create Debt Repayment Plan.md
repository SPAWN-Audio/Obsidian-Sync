# Brief

> [!example] #### Goal 3 - 💸 Create & Implement Debt Repayment Plan
> - By Month 6, I will have a clear, written debt repayment plan in place — prioritised correctly, with realistic targets based on actual income at that point — and will have made at least one proactive contact with a creditor or debt support service to begin formally addressing the situation.

- **ADHD adjustment:** A debt repayment plan only works when income exists — so this goal is deliberately placed at Month 6, giving time to face the picture (Goal 1) and stabilise spending (Goal 2) first, while also allowing time to begin generating income through the Career goals running in parallel. Sequencing matters enormously here.
- **Visualise it:** Picture a creditor marking one of your debts as "arrangement in place" — that status change is enormous. It stops escalation, often freezes interest, and means you are no longer avoiding, you are _actively resolving_. That shift in identity — from someone drowning to someone swimming — changes everything.
- **Manageable steps:**
    - Month 3–4: Contact StepChange or National Debtline for a free debt review — they will help you understand your options (Debt Management Plan, IVA, debt consolidation, or simply negotiating directly with creditors)
    - Month 5: Prioritise debts correctly — priority debts first (rent arrears, council tax, utilities, court fines), then non-priority (credit cards, personal loans, overdrafts)
    - Month 6: Use the "snowball method" (pay smallest debt first for psychological wins) or "avalanche method" (pay highest interest first to save money) — choose whichever keeps you motivated
    - Month 7–12: Make every agreed payment, however small. A £5/month arrangement is infinitely better than nothing — it shows good faith and stops escalation
- **Potential problem:** Feeling like the debt is so large that any effort is pointless — this is the most dangerous thought in debt recovery and it is not true. Every creditor would rather have a small consistent payment than nothing. Every pound paid is a pound gone. Every month in an arrangement is a month not escalating.
- **Key resources — all free:**
    - **StepChange** — stepchange.org / 0800 138 1111
    - **National Debtline** — nationaldebtline.org / 0808 808 4000
    - **Citizens Advice** — citizensadvice.org.uk
    - **MoneyHelper** — moneyhelper.org.uk
- **Reward:** When you make your first arranged debt payment — no matter how small — mark it. That is the moment you stopped running and started resolving. It deserves to be acknowledged as the turning point it genuinely is.
