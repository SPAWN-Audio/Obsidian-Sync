# Brief 

> [!example] #### Goal 3 - 💸 Land First 3 Paid Gigs
> - By December 2026, I will secure at least 3 paid audio projects — whether film sound, game audio, or sound design — even if small or low-budget, generating initial freelance income and real-world professional experience.

- **ADHD adjustment:** "Get freelance clients" is too vague and easy to procrastinate on forever. This goal is anchored to a specific number (3 gigs) with a clear pipeline so you always know the next action, not just the end goal.
- **Visualise it:** Picture receiving your first payment notification for audio work — proof that your skills have real-world value. That first one changes everything.
- **Manageable steps:**
    - Month 1–2: Identify 3 platforms to find entry-level work (e.g. Fiverr, Upwork, indie game forums like itch.io, student film groups on Facebook)
    - Month 3–4: Create a simple 1-page rate card and a short pitch template you can personalise quickly
    - Month 5–6: Apply for or pitch at least 2 projects per week — volume matters early on
    - Month 7+: Deliver gig 1, request a testimonial, use it in all future pitches
- **Potential problem:** Undercharging or overcommitting due to excitement — set a minimum rate before you start (even if low) and a maximum project scope per gig while freelancing part-time.
- **Reward:** Put 10% of every paid gig toward a dedicated "gear/plugin fund" — your income directly upgrading your craft is deeply motivating.
