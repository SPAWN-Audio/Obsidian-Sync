# Principle

The standard for this project is _**completion**_ **over** _**perfection**_.

The purpose is not to create three flawless tracks, but to train consistency, build trust in creative judgment, and make finishing a natural part of the workflow.

---

# Overview

> _This project is a structured self-directed deliberate practice plan. Running from: **May 18 to June 16**_

Designed to break the habit of leaving tracks unfinished by completing:

- **one mix-ready master every 10 days** across **three focused cycles**.

Rather than optimising for perfection on any single piece, the project is built to strengthen consistency, completion, and creative momentum.

The core aim is to develop a reliable production rhythm that turns finishing into a repeatable habit instead of an occasional outcome.

---

# Objective

> _Build the capacity to move from idea to completed master with greater speed, clarity, and confidence._

#### By the end of the 30-day period, success looks like:

- Completing **three finished tracks.**
- As close to a full Mix & Master by the end of each cycle.

#### Skills improved over the project

- reducing hesitation and over-editing during production
- improving critical listening and mix decision-making
- establishing a personal workflow that feels intuitive and repeatable

---

# Structure

> _The project is divided into **three 10-day cycles**, each resulting in one completed track. Every cycle follows the same three-phase arc, or something close to it:_

- **Explore (Days 1–3):** generate ideas, sketch direction, select the strongest concept
- **Build (Days 4–7):** develop arrangement, sound design, core production, and mix direction
- **Finish (Days 8–10):** commit to final decisions, complete the mix, export masters, and reflect

```
This repeated structure is intended to reduce decision fatigue and create a dependable cadence from ideation through delivery.
```

---

# Deliverables

> _At the end of each cycle, the required outputs are:_

- 1x **stereo master** in **WAV** format
- Uploaded to a SAMPLY project
- 1x short **retrospective note** capturing what worked, what slowed progress, and what should change in the next cycle
    - _This reflective part can & should be as simple as a 1-2 sentences / dot points_

---

# Success Criteria

> _The project will be considered successful if, by the target date:_

- All three cycles are completed
- Each cycle ends with a delivered master and retrospective
- The production process feels clearer and faster than it did at the start, or at the very least more insights on where improvements can be made.
- Creative energy is being spent on decisions that move tracks forward rather than on unnecessary reopening of finished work
